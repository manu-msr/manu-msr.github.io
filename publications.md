**Leetris: Un videojuego de apoyo al desarrollo de habilidades de comprensión de lectura** ([pdf](publicaciones/publicacion_2016_01.pdf))

Manuel Soto Romero, Yoshua Ian Alfaro Mendoza, and David Alejandro Nieto Andrade. Advances in Human-Computer Interaction, Vol. 1, No. 1, pp. 30–32, September 21, 2016
DOI: [https://doi.org/10.47756/aihc.y1i1.8](https://doi.org/10.47756/aihc.y1i1.8)

This paper presents the development of *Leetris*, an educational video game designed to improve reading comprehension skills in high school students. Based on gamification principles, Leetris combines two parallel cognitive activities: reading short texts and solving interactive challenges, with the aim of keeping the player engaged and promoting active learning. The game is structured into progressively difficult levels, adapting to the user's progress and promoting a personalized learning experience. This proposal seeks to offer an innovative tool that integrates digital entertainment with specific educational objectives.

**Seguridad de sistemas de tipos vía Verificación de Modelos** ([pdf](publicaciones/publicacion_2023_01.pdf))

Manuel Soto Romero. National Autonomous University of Mexico, Graduate Program in Computer Science and Engineering, Institute for Research in Applied Mathematics and Systems (IIMAS), 2023.

This work presents a proposal to automate the verification of type system safety in programming languages using the *Glass Box Model Checking* technique. This technique helps mitigate the state explosion problem by generating search spaces through constraints and SAT solvers, and applies a pruning process to optimize the analysis. The implementation was developed in the functional language Haskell, resulting in a model checker capable of analyzing preservation and progress properties on test programming languages. The work was supported by PAPIIT projects (IN119920, IN101723) and a CONACyT scholarship. The thesis aims to contribute an efficient and practical approach to the formal verification of safety properties in type systems.

**Manual de Prácticas para la Asignatura de Programación Declarativa** ([pdf](publicaciones/publicacion_2019_01.pdf))

Manuel Soto Romero. National Autonomous University of Mexico, Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Science, Faculty of Science, 2019.

This undergraduate project presents a manual designed to complement the teaching of the Declarative Programming course in the Computer Science program. The manual offers a series of exercises covering the logic and functional programming paradigms, using languages such as Prolog, Haskell, and Racket. Each exercise includes objectives, a theoretical introduction, tasks, and expected results, facilitating the practical application of theoretical concepts. The material aims to strengthen students’ understanding of declarative programming, promoting active and autonomous learning. Furthermore, the manual was developed as part of the PAPIME PE102117 project, focused on innovation and improvement of teaching at UNAM.
